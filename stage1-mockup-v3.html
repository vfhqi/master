Stage 1 — Consolidating and/or Basing

Master Dashboard V2 · mockup v3 · 22 real stocks · pipeline data 12-May-26 18:02
Mockup only. Standalone HTML for spec sign-off — not wired into the live dashboard. Nav links to other tabs are placeholders until those tabs are built.
Stage 1 looks for stocks whose long-term trend has stopped going down and started to flatten. The aim is to spot bases before a Stage 2 uptrend kicks off — the earliest, highest-conviction point to consider initiating a position. Eight separate tests across four groups; the more tests a stock passes, the more likely it has genuinely entered a consolidation phase rather than a temporary pause in a downtrend.

G1 · Slowing decline Is the long-term moving average still falling, but falling more slowly each month? A decelerating decline is the first hint of a bottom forming. If a moving average is already flat or rising (*), the relevant Group 2 test fires twice instead.
G2 · Flat moving averages Has the long-term trend genuinely stalled? Both 150-day and 200-day moving averages need to be within ±2% of where they were one, two, and three months ago. Flat moving averages are the clearest mechanical signature of a base.
G3 · Moving average stack Are shorter moving averages now sitting above longer ones? 50-day above 150-day (within 3%) and 150-day above 200-day (within 3%) show price has begun to lift the recent trend above the longer one — typical of a late base or early Stage 2 transition.
G4 · Higher lows Are recent pullback lows higher than earlier ones? The most recent monthly low should be above the prior three monthly lows (1-month test); the most recent three-monthly low should be above the prior three (3-month test). Higher lows are the buyer-stepping-in signal.

Spec recap

Rating thresholds: Possible 2/8 · Plausible 3/8 · Probable Early 4/8 · Probable Late 5+/8. Inside Probable Late the pill darkens 5→6→7→8.

Default sort: Score descending then ticker ascending. Click any column to override.

Number formats: integers with thousands separators (#,##0); negatives in parentheses (#,##0); 0 dp above 100, 1 dp above 20, 2 dp below 20. Percentages 0 dp with (0)% for negatives.

Sticky headers: group header and column header rows both pin to the top while you scroll the table body.

Colour-coding: "Colour by" tints the left edge by industry or sector group. "Portfolio tint" colours the right edge of every row that's in your live positions or in the same sector/industry/peer/cohort. The two are independent and visually separated (left vs right edge).

This pattern propagates: Stage 2/3/4, Indicators, Setups, Tests, and Master Overview all use the same chrome — controls, tiles, captions, sticky headers, sort, persistence, scope, colour. Each tab swaps in its own groups + tests + threshold ladder.
